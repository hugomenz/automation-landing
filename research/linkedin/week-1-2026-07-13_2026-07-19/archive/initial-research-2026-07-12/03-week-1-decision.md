# Week-1 decision & plan — Hugo Menz LinkedIn visibility

**Research date:** 2026-07-12 · **Execution week:** 2026-07-13 → 2026-07-19 · **Skill:** linkedin-copilot · **Mode today:** research only, nothing published.

## How this decision was made
Not by picking the segment with the most companies or followers. It weighs commercial
fit, *real* activity, presence of buyers, access to conversations, offer relevance, Hugo's
credible experience, and probability of reaching the right audience. The honest constraint:
the machine-builder SMEs with the best commercial fit are **largely inactive on LinkedIn**,
while the most *active* on-topic accounts are **CPQ software vendors and consultants** whose
audiences may not be buyers. Week 1 is therefore a **test**, not a rollout.

## Mandatory decisions
1. **Primary industrial segment:** **End-of-Line / secondary packaging / palletizing machinery** (Verpackungstechnik + EOL). Best offer fit (configurable, repeatable, requirement-heavy quoting) and at least some active accounts (Prewa, EOL Experts).
2. **Secondary industrial segment:** **Sondermaschinenbau + handling/assembly** (Schnaithmann, ASYS, VS, Noll, FORM+TEST…). Highest density of reachable decision-makers; low LinkedIn activity → outreach/watch, not comments.
3. **Ecosystem / audience group:** **Angebotsprozess / CPQ & engineering-efficiency conversation** (dima, Perspectix, camos, Schepers) + **industrial media** (Konradin). High distribution value, but **amber** — watch for software/consultant skew.

## Scale reality (must read)
The brief's 250–350 companies / 300–500 people **conflict with the skill you told me to use**
(`linkedin-copilot`: 30 companies / 60 people / 25 posts; "quality over list size") and with
your own rules (no invented data, conservative pace, no infinite scroll). Reaching those
numbers honestly in one read-only session is impossible, so I delivered a **real, verified,
de-duplicated core** (~42 orgs, ~38 people, ~12 posts) with `unknown` wherever a field was not
opened — and documented the repeatable searches to grow it during the week. Nothing was invented.

## Final result

1. **Main decision:** Run Week 1 as a *test of audience reachability*, not a volume push. Contribute a few specific, human comments in the End-of-Line + Angebotsprozess conversations and publish 2 own posts; measure whether **buyers**, not just software people, respond.
2. **Primary / secondary segment:** Primary = End-of-Line / packaging / palletizing. Secondary = Sondermaschinenbau + handling/assembly.
3. **Priority audience:** BW/Bayern Vertriebsleiter, Leiter Technischer Vertrieb, Leiter Projektierung, and GF of machine-builder SMEs; plus the Angebotsprozess/CPQ voices for distribution.
4. **What was discarded:** exact multiword/location company searches (zero results); generic-AI influencers, marketing/video lead-gen, sales-coaching consultants, recruiters, off-topic fairs; large non-SME accounts as prospects (kept only as audience).
5. **Hypothesis that was too optimistic:** that 250–350 qualified companies and 300–500 people are discoverable and verifiable in one honest session — and that the machine builders themselves are active on LinkedIn. Both proved false.
6. **What has enough evidence to test:** the End-of-Line + Angebotsprozess conversation exists and is active (Prewa, EOL Experts, dima, Perspectix, Schepers) — enough to run a real commenting test this week.
7. **Risks:** (a) the active audience is software/consultants, not buyers (amber→red risk); (b) buyer profiles are inactive, so comments won't reach them; (c) over-commenting/account friction — mitigated by ≤3/day, ≤15/week, human approval each time; (d) inferred company slugs need confirming before outreach.
8. **Exact Monday actions:** (i) read Prewa post + draft/approve comment #1; (ii) verify 30/60/90-day activity of the top-12 people; (iii) open Erdinc Erboral's activity; (iv) build a week-2 watchlist (no auto-connect). See [03-week-1-action-queue.csv](03-week-1-action-queue.csv).
9. **Questions to answer by Friday:** Do buyers (not software/consultants) react or reply? Do any priority builder accounts post at all? Which single search yields the most active buyers? Did any profile visit come from a target company?
10. **Thresholds:** **Continue** the segment if ≥2 buyer/problem-owner replies or ≥1 qualified conversation. **Switch segment** (to pure Sondermaschinenbau outreach) if visibility is real but only among software/consultants. **Reduce LinkedIn to a credibility channel** (profile + occasional post, effort elsewhere) if there are no relevant replies and the buyer segment is inactive.
